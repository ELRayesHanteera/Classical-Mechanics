Goldstein 3e — Chapter 1, Problem 13

Derive the rocket equation; integrate for constant burn rate; estimate fuel-to-empty weight ratio for escape velocity.

Book p. 31 (PDF p. 50)

1. Physical setup

         |

         |
      ─── ground ───

Part C data: $v' = 2.1$ km/s, $|\dot m| = m_0/60$ s$^{-1}$, start from rest, target $v_{\text{esc}} \approx 11.2$ km/s.

2. Intuition

Skateboard analogy: Throw bricks backward → you roll forward. A rocket throws exhaust continuously, but gravity pulls down the whole time.

Not fixed mass: You cannot use $F=ma$ with constant $m$. Mass leaves every second.

What Goldstein asks:

  1. A: Derive $m\dot v = -v'\dot m - mg$ from momentum.
  2. B: Integrate for constant $\dot m = -m_0/60$.
  3. C: How much fuel vs empty rocket for escape speed?

Why momentum? Thrust is momentum carried away by exhaust. Energy works too, but momentum tracks thrust directly.

Sign trap: $dm/dt < 0$ when fuel burns, so $-v'\dot m > 0$ (thrust upward). ✓

3. Formal solution — Part A

Principle: Conservation of momentum + external gravitational impulse.

Before / after a burn $dt$

Before: mass $m$, velocity $v$, momentum $mv$.

After: rocket $(m+dm)(v+dv)$; ejected gas mass $-dm$ at lab velocity $v - v'$.

$$p' = (m+dm)(v+dv) + (-dm)(v-v')$$

External impulse from gravity: $J = -mg\,dt$. So $p' = mv - mg\,dt$.

Expand and simplify

$$(m+dm)(v+dv) = mv + m\,dv + v\,dm + dm\,dv$$

$$(-dm)(v-v') = -dm\,v + dm\,v'$$

The $v\,dm$ terms cancel. Drop $dm\,dv$:

$$m\,dv + v'\,dm = -mg\,dt$$

$$\boxed{m\frac{dv}{dt} = -v'\frac{dm}{dt} - mg}$$

Reading: Thrust $-v'\dot m$ (upward since $\dot m<0$) minus weight $mg$.

Limit $g=0$: standard free-space rocket equation. ✓

3. Formal solution — Part B

Given $\dot m = -m_0/60$. Define $\mu = m_0/60 = -\dot m$.

Chain rule: $m\,\frac{dv}{dm}\frac{dm}{dt} = v'\frac{dm}{dt} - mg$ → $m\,\frac{dv}{dm} = v' - \frac{g}{\mu}m$.

Integrate from $(m_0, v_0)$ to $(m,v)$:

$$\boxed{v(m) = v_0 - v'\ln\!\left(\frac{m}{m_0}\right) + \frac{g}{\mu}(m-m_0)}$$

With $v_0=0$ and $\mu = m_0/60$:

$$\boxed{v(m) = v'\ln\!\left(\frac{m_0}{m}\right) - \frac{60g}{m_0}(m_0-m)}$$

Thrust benefit (logarithmic) minus gravity penalty (linear in fuel used).

3. Formal solution — Part C

At burnout $m = m_e$, let $r = m_f/m_e$:

$$\boxed{v_{\text{burn}} = v'\ln(r+1) - \frac{60g\,r}{r+1}}$$

Set $v_{\text{burn}} = v_{\text{esc}} = 11.2\times10^3$ m/s with $v'=2.1\times10^3$ m/s, $g=9.8$ m/s$^2$:

Crude estimate ($m_0 \approx m_f$): $v_{\text{esc}} \approx v'\ln r - 60g$ → $r \approx e^{(11200+588)/2100} \approx 274$.

Exact: $\boxed{m_f/m_e \approx 273}$ (Goldstein: "almost 300").

Most thrust accelerates unburned fuel; gravity acts the entire burn.

5. Takeaway

Variable mass → momentum conservation, not naive $F=ma$. Thrust is $-v'\dot m$. Escape from rest needs fuel weight ~270–300× empty rocket. Habit: isolate a small $dt$, write before/after momenta, identify external impulses.
